Solving today's greatest health challenges requires a new generation of biomedical engineers. An aging U.S. population is driving demand for better, more cost-effective medical devices. Recent high-profile reports on failure rates in artificial hips underline the critical need for improved engineering and materials design of long-lasting devices.

Our interdisciplinary Master of Science (MS) program in Bioengineering is designed for students with a BS in engineering or physics* who would like to continue or move their career in the direction of Bioengineering; students who would like to strengthen their academic credentials/portfolio prior to applying to medical school; and workers in the biotech industry who would like to strengthen their technical background, broaden future employment opportunities, and/or re-direct their specific expertise.
